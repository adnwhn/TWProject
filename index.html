Second Hand, Second Love

Usage condition grading terminology:

  1. M (mint) - Absolutely perfect in every way. Never been played and usually sealed. Often rumored but rarely seen, Mint should never be used as a grade unless more than one person agrees that the record or sleeve truly is in this condition.
  2. NM (near mint) - The record looks like it's been opened for the first time. Glossy disc which clearly has only been played a few times. There are no marks on it and the whole package is complete.
  3. VG (very good) - More obvious flaws, but still usable. A little rub, light visible scratches or marks, a seam split. When played, it has surface noise, and some scratches may be audible, especially in soft passages and during a song's intro and ending. But the noise will not overpower the music otherwise.
  4. G (good) - Good does not mean bad! The record still plays through without skipping, so it can serve as a filler song until something better comes along. But it has significant surface noise and groove wear.
  5. F (fair) - Expect major noise issues, skipping or repeating. The record is cracked, warped and have deep scratches. The cover are also wrecked. Only the most outrageously rare items ever sell for more than a few cents in this condition.

Trivia: How to calculate the surface of a disc?

Generally, there are 2 types of vinyl records: 33 RPM and 45 RPM. Their dimensions are 18 cm and 30 cm respectively. These lengths correspond to the diametral length of a circle (D), which helps us to calculate the radius:

R = D 2

The formula for area of a circle is:

A = π × R 2

Just replace π with its approximate value (3.14159...) and there you have it, the surface of a vinyl disc!
